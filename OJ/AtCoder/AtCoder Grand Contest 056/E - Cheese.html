問題文

すぬけくんは今から，N−1N-1N−1 回チーズを投げます． 具体的には，以下のような操作を N−1N-1N−1 回繰り返します．

  • その後，座標 yyy からチーズを投げる． チーズは，円周上を時計回りに移動する． ネズミのいる位置をチーズが通る時，以下のことが起こる．
    • そのネズミが今までにチーズを食べていない場合：

Problem Statement

There is a circumference of length NNN. On this circumference, a point whose distance from a certain fixed point, measured in the clockwise direction, is xxx has the coordinate xxx.

For each integer iii (0≤i≤N−10 \leq i \leq N-10≤i≤N−1), there is a mouse at coordinate i+0.5i+0.5i+0.5.

Snuke will throw a piece of cheese N−1N-1N−1 times. More specifically, he repeats the following N−1N-1N−1 times.

  • Choose an integer yyy (0≤y≤N−10 \leq y \leq N-10≤y≤N−1) randomly, where y=iy=iy=i is chosen with probability AiA_iAi​ percent. This choice is made independently each time.
  • Then, throw cheese from the coordinate yyy. The cheese travels in the clockwise direction on the circumference. When the cheese meets a mouse, the following happens.
    • If the mouse has not eaten cheese:
      • It eats the cheese with probability 1/21/21/2. The cheese disappears.
      • It does nothing with probability 1/21/21/2.
    • If the mouse has already eaten cheese:
      • It does nothing.
  • The cheese keeps traveling on the circumference until eaten by some mouse.

After N−1N-1N−1 throws of cheese, there will be exactly one mouse that has never eaten cheese. For each k=0,1,⋯ ,N−1k=0,1,\cdots,N-1k=0,1,⋯,N−1, find the probability that the mouse at coordinate k+0.5k+0.5k+0.5 ends up not eating cheese, modulo 998244353998244353998244353.

How to represent a probability modulo 998244353998244353998244353

Thus, there is a unique integer RRR such that R×Q≡P(mod998244353),0≤R<998244353R \times Q \equiv P \pmod{998244353}, 0 \leq R < 998244353R×Q≡P(mod998244353),0≤R<998244353. Answer with this RRR.

y=1y=1y=1 is always chosen. When throwing cheese from this point, the following happens.

  • With probability 1/21/21/2, the mouse at coordinate 1.51.51.5 eats it.
  • With probability 1/41/41/4, the mouse at coordinate 0.50.50.5 eats it.
  • With probability 1/81/81/8, the mouse at coordinate 1.51.51.5 eats it.
  • With probability 1/161/161/16, the mouse at coordinate 0.50.50.5 eats it.

In the end, the mice at coordinates 0.50.50.5 and 1.51.51.5 eat this cheese with probabilities 1/31/31/3 and 2/32/32/3, respectively.

Therefore, they end up not eating cheese with probabilities 2/32/32/3 and 1/31/31/3, respectively.
